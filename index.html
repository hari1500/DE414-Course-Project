Seven C's

  • Cause

    Every design project is usually initiated by a need (be it a commercial requirement or a social necessity) or a problem with the product. This need or problem recognition is a key trigger for the project

  • Context

    To study and understand the existing product or similar products in the market, the product scenario, its users, and pros and cons of the product as experienced by them. Studying the available data and through interaction with all the users and stakeholders is involved here

  • Comprehension

    The designer analyses all the information gathered and methodically lists out his observations and perceptions of the issues involved and the causes behind. The design insights thus arrived form the basis for the design intervention

  • Check

    The check is essential to understand what requirements the product should ultimately cater to and what problems it should resolve. The check, which is a product brief is created from the design insights and is approved by the client

  • Conception

    Using the check list the designer arrives at multiple ideas to resolve the problems listed. This is a very important stage which starts with conceiving ideas, grouping them into clusters, arriving at multiple concepts and finally zeroing in on one all encompassing concept

  • Crafting

    The final concept is further refined against various criteria like use of material and manufacturing limitations. The final product design is showcased by creating small scale mock-up models, CAD models or working prototypes. The final product which meets the product brief conditions is then taken up for mass production.

  • Connection

    The last but not the least important concern is the connection, the new product makes with the consumer. In fact it is the customer satisfaction that makes the winning product and completes the most important criterion for innovation

Cause



One day I and all of my cousins went to my Grandparents’ home after coming back from IIT Bombay hostel during the pandemic. Since it’s the return of all the grandchildren after a long time, my grandmother made a lot of varieties to eat. Although she seemed fine at the moment, she felt a lot of pain in the back, legs before going to sleep. Then she told standing and working for long hours in the kitchen would cause such problems. Then she also mentioned the kitchen is too hot while cooking

After thinking about this we did some googling and found out that "Typical Indians spend over 13.2 hours a week cooking whereas The global cooking average is just under 6.5 hours a week according to Daily News". We think a typical housewife from rural areas in India spend more time than the above artical claims

Due to problems in the kitchen, it has been shown to have a number of potentially serious health outcomes, such as back and leg pain, headache, cardiovascular problems, stress, dehydration, fatigue. And some pregnancy-related problems in case of housewives

So we are working on the "Problems faced by the Elderly in the Kitchen"

Context



The problem revolves around(simply problem-space is) the elderly and housewives working in the kitchen. So we interviewed few elderly people and asked the following questions

  • Where do you feel pain due to prolonged standing in the kitchen?
  • How kitchen atmosphere affects the cooking performance?
  • What do you do for pain relief?
  • Any other problems do you face in kitchen?

Existing Solutions:

  • For standing problems, a stool is introduced. Which is suitable especially for kids and not suitable for elder people. It also wont address back pain and many other problems
  • To remove hot air while cooking, exhaust is used
  • For easy accessesing of kitchen supplies, these slidable doors are used
  • Some people directly sit along with their cooking apparatus which is stationed on the low level or ground

None of the existing solutions solves all the problems faced by our primary users



Comprehension



Based on the case studies, we deduced the following:

  • The main problem we found out from the case-study is the leg and back pain due to prolonged standing
  • Hot conditions in kitchen environment often leads to excess sweating and also causes mild headache and fatigue, which affects the cooking performance
  • For pain relief, they take some rest or used pain relief creams
  • Though the users didn't explicitly mentioned about the problem of accessing kitchen supplies and cleaning the kitchen after cooking. We found that problem while observing the user in the context

Our Approach:

  • We are thinking of a chair type or bar stool type support for sitting or leaning respectively. Along with a lever for height adjustment
  • Assist for back support
  • A fan to provide cool air to user
  • One handle of chair is replaced with
  • A brush for cleaning the kitchen ( to be fitted on chair)

Check



Our checklist includes:

  • Modular design
  • Should give back support and less strain to user
  • Should address hot conditions of kitchen
  • Easy accessibility of kitchen ingredients/supplies
  • Strong, Durable and Robust
  • Light weight for portability
  • Low space occupancy for small kitchens
  • Multiple sizes of seat because of different peoples


Conception



We thought of several ideas for the device. We are listing some of the top ideas here with pros and cons

Concepts:

  1. Simple Chair Model
    Pros
    • Back support available
    • Strong and durable
    • Can be used for long cooking sessions
    Cons
    • Not cost-friendly because this involves multiple parts
    • Occupies a lot of space
    • Hard to cook if cooking involves repeated standing and sitting

  2. Rod Model
    Pros
    • User can lean onto the Rod instead of sitting and standing repeatedly
    • Strong and durable
    Cons
    • Not portable and hard to move to other places
    • Can be used only for short cooking sessions

  3. X-shaped bottom Model
    Pros
    • Very compact because of foldable design
    • Back support available
    • Strong and durable
    Cons
    • Not cost-friendly because involves a metallic frame
    • Hard to cook if cooking involves repeated standing and sitting

  4. Reverse Leg Model
    Pros
    • Very compact because of foldable design similar to a leg
    Cons
    • No back support
    • No comfort

  5. Bus stop leaner Model
    Pros
    • Users can lean onto the Rod instead of sitting and standing repeatedly. User friendly for both long and short cooking sessions
    • Cost-effective since crafting can be done with any material (like plastic and wood)
    • Occupies low space in kitchen and portable to use
    Cons
    • No back support
    • Prolonged leaning might lead to pain in the ankles

We chose Bus Stop Leaner Model
